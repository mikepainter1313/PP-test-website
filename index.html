What I Stand For

FOUR WORDS. NON-NEGOTIABLE.

These aren't aspirational. They're not a brand exercise. They're the values I've held throughout a twenty-year career — and the ones I use to decide who I work with, and how I work with them.

INTEGRITY

I do what I say I will do. I say what I actually think. I've turned down opportunities that didn't sit right, and I've walked away from situations that asked me to compromise what I believed. I'd rather be honest and trusted than comfortable and quiet.

INCLUSION

Twenty years in education taught me that talent is everywhere — and that systems often fail the people who need them most. I've spent my career building environments where everyone has a real shot. I bring that same lens to every organisation I work with.

KINDNESS

Not softness. Kindness. There's a difference. The best leaders I've known — and the best organisations I've built — were places where people felt genuinely cared for. That's not a nice-to-have. It's a performance strategy. Kind cultures are resilient cultures.

CREATIVITY

I've never met a problem I couldn't find a new way into. Creativity isn't just what I do — it's how I think. I bring it to strategy, to systems, to data, to people. The most elegant solution is rarely the obvious one. I like finding the one nobody thought of.
